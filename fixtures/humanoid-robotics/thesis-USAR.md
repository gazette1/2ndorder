# USAR: federally funded magnet capacity with dated 2027 milestones, priced on execution not on today's revenue

## Bear case first
The numbers under the story are thin and the insiders have been net sellers. Trailing 12-month revenue is $1.6MM against a $297.6MM net loss (XBRL), so every dollar of the thesis is forward-dated. Insiders sold a net $31.1MM over the last year (Form 4), dominated by a Director disposing of 2.1MM shares at $15.75 in August 2025. Federal customer proof is one $99M Department of Defense contract (USASpending), not the multi-hundred-million funding headline. Disbursements depend on purchase commitments that may not convert to recognized sales [5], and the government can claw back the new projects if they do not commence by mid 2027 [19].

## Business today
The company is pre-commercial: $1.6MM revenue, a $297.6MM net loss, and $360MM of cash on the balance sheet as of December 2025 (XBRL). It is building domestic rare earth capacity across five awarded projects, including two magnet plants and two strip-cast neodymium-iron-boron metal plants [9] [18], with commercial volume still being contracted through purchase letters of intent rather than recognized sales [11] [12].

## Chain position
Enabler, rare earth magnet supply. High torque density joint motors depend on neodymium magnets, and a humanoid platform carries kilograms of magnet material across 30 to 40 joints. Magnet producers are paid per unit by every robot OEM regardless of which platform wins.

## Customer graph
Customer proof today is one Department of Defense contract worth $99M (USASpending, government buyer), plus purchase commitments that are contracted but not yet recognized as revenue [5] [6]. No other public filer names the company as a supplier in the last two years (reverse-citation returned none), which is consistent with a pre-commercial producer that has not yet shipped at volume. The buyer base is government and prospective, not a proven commercial book.

## Insider and management signal
Net insider activity is negative, a $31.1MM sale versus $2.2MM of buying (Form 4), which reads as post-transaction distribution rather than conviction. The offsetting detail: the most recent director action was an open-market purchase of 100,000 shares at $21.44 in January 2026, above the $15.75 August sale price. Management commitment shows up instead in the funding agreement obligations rather than in narrative [2] [5].

## Non-linear case
The company is adding magnet capacity that does not exist in its current revenue base, backed by a federal Direct Funding Agreement [15] [8]. Disbursement milestones require minimum cumulative magnet purchase commitments, so contract wins are observable in filings as they happen [5] [6]. If humanoid deployment begins inside 2027 to 2030, it arrives as incremental demand on top of the defense and EV demand the funding already presumes, against capacity committed today [2] [19]. Success is a multiple of the current business because the current business is $1.6MM of revenue.

## Evidence
- Disbursements are gated on minimum cumulative Magnet Purchase Commitments [5], delivered to the Department within five business days of execution [6].
- Magnet Project 2 must commence by June 30, 2027 and Metal Project 2 by September 30, 2027 [19].
- Products are defined as rare-earth magnets and strip-cast neodymium-iron-boron metal, plus heavy rare earth oxides from Round Top [18].
- Magnet Project 2 is a new magnet manufacturing facility [15], one of two Additional Projects [8].
- Three of five projects commenced before their clawback dates [2].

## What would change our mind
- A missed commencement clawback date for either Additional Project, visible in an 8-K amendment to the funding agreement [19].
- A shift from net insider selling to sustained open-market buying by officers, visible in Form 4.
- Purchase commitment disclosures naming robotics customers, or new federal awards on USASpending beyond the current $99M contract.
