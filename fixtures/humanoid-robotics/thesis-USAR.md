# USAR: federally funded magnet capacity with dated 2027 milestones, levered to humanoid unit volumes

## Bear case first
Every excerpt comes from a financing agreement, not from revenue. Disbursements depend on purchase commitments that may not convert to recognized sales [5], Magnet Project 2 still needs its power infrastructure plan resolved [1], and the government can claw back awards if the new projects do not commence by mid 2027 [19]. Nothing in the filings ties demand to humanoid robotics specifically; the magnets serve defense and EV demand first, and a humanoid delay to the 2030s leaves the thesis paying for capacity the theme never fills. Execution risk on four simultaneous plant builds is not addressed by the excerpts at all.

## Business today
The company is building domestic rare earth capacity across five awarded projects: the Round Top mine, two strip-cast neodymium-iron-boron metal plants, and two rare earth magnet plants [9] [18]. Commercial volume is still being contracted through pre-award and post-award purchase letters of intent rather than recognized sales [11] [12].

## Chain position
Enabler, rare earth magnet supply. High torque density joint motors depend on neodymium magnets, and a humanoid platform carries kilograms of magnet material across 30 to 40 joints. Magnet producers are paid per unit by every robot OEM regardless of which platform wins.

## Non-linear case
The company is adding magnet manufacturing capacity that does not exist in its current revenue base, backed by a federal Direct Funding Agreement [15] [8]. Disbursement milestones require minimum cumulative magnet purchase commitments, so contract wins are observable in filings as they happen [5] [6]. If humanoid deployment begins inside 2027 to 2030, it arrives as incremental demand on top of the defense and EV demand the funding already presumes, against capacity that is committed today [2] [19]. Success is a multiple of the current business because the current business is still pre-commercial.

## Filing evidence
- Three of five projects commenced before their clawback dates [2].
- Magnet Project 2 must commence by June 30, 2027 and Metal Project 2 by September 30, 2027 [19].
- Disbursements are gated on minimum cumulative Magnet Purchase Commitments [5], delivered to the Department within five business days of execution [6].
- Products are defined as rare-earth magnets and strip-cast neodymium-iron-boron metal, plus heavy rare earth oxides from Round Top [18].
- Magnet Project 2 is a new magnet manufacturing facility [15], one of two Additional Projects [8], with its power plan a disbursement condition [1].

## What would change our mind
- A missed commencement clawback date for either Additional Project, visible in an 8-K amendment to the funding agreement [19].
- Purchase commitment disclosures naming robotics customers, or failing to grow along the milestone schedule, visible in 8-K exhibits [5].
- A 10-K that shows magnet revenue concentrating entirely in defense programs, which would decouple the name from the humanoid theme.
