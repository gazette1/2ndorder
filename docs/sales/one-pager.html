Corollary Research preview

One scenario in. Every consequence out, cited to the filing sentence.

Corollary is a scenario consequence-mapping engine for small and mid-cap buy-side equity research. A PM enters a thesis in plain language. The engine maps what follows from it, checks every claim against SEC filings, and returns the names a SMID fund can actually own.

How it works
01

Enter a scenario

A macro thesis, a news event, or a pasted article link, in plain language. No ticker required.

02

The engine maps the consequences

First, second, and third order effects, beneficiaries and at-risk names, drawn as a navigable tree. Every node is checked against SEC filings: the engine searches EDGAR full text, reads the actual 10-Ks, and cites the exact filing sentences behind each claim. No claim without a source sentence. Every bull map gets a bear map automatically, so you see what breaks the thesis.

03

Leave with an IC-ready memo

Long and short candidates, each thesis drafted bear-case-first, with filing citations attached.

Built for SMID funds specifically

Size-aware ranking

Hits are sized against market cap, so mega-caps do not crowd out under-covered small and mid caps. Each node ranks the names where the consequence is material.

Liquidity reality checks

Every candidate is screened for dollar average daily volume, days to build a position, net cash, runway, dilution risk, and shelf registrations on file.

Filing-sentence citations

Each consequence links to the exact sentences in the 10-K that support it. You can read the primary source in one click.

Counter-scenario by default

The bear map is generated alongside the bull map, not on request. The memo leads with what would have to be false.

Corpus: 5,449 US-listed SEC filers read from their 10-K, or the S-1 for pre-IPO names, refreshed as filings land. Each candidate also carries its 8-K events, 13D/13G holders, proxy read, insider record including Form 144 notices, and a sector-regulator check (FDA, FCC, or FERC). Market caps are delayed price times SEC-reported shares. Corollary is not a licensed market data feed and not investment advice.

The ask: 30 minutes, your live thesis

Corollary is a research preview offered to a small number of design-partner funds. Bring a thesis you are working on now to a 30-minute session and watch the engine map it while you ask questions.
