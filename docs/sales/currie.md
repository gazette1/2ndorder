sole PM since October 1, 2025. He is the person who would hire the associate
analyst seat you built Corollary to make a case for. That makes this the one

## The map excerpt (real run, tuned to his lane)

Scenario run: "Vertical software platforms embed payments and AI-driven
financial automation into small and mid-sized business workflows, expanding
revenue per customer and addressable market." 14 map nodes, counter-scenario
run automatically.

The on-thesis names, real run, every claim filing-cited:

- **i3 Verticals (IIIV), about $580MM.** The lead. Vertical software for
  public-sector entities (courts, public safety, administration) with a
  proprietary payment facilitator embedded in the workflows. Filing sentence:
  "Our proprietary payment facilitator platform seamlessly integrates into
  our software solutions." Revenue growing about 11 percent, four trading days
  to build a position, no reality flags. This is the under-covered small-cap
  the thesis predicts, and the one to lead the note with.
- **Intapp (INTA), about $2.1B.** Higher quality, better covered, so name it
  as validation of the method rather than as a hidden gem. Vertical AI
  software for accounting, investment banking, private capital, legal, and
  real-assets firms. Filing sentence: "Intapp is a leading global provider of
  AI-powered solutions for the world's premier accounting, consulting,
  investment banking, legal, private capital and real assets firms." Growing
  about 17 percent. This is the finance-industry vertical, the direct line to
  his Future of Finance fund.
- **Crexendo (CXDO), about $254MM.** A smaller, more speculative cloud-
  communications name the map surfaced; carries a dilution flag (share count
  up about 12 percent). Mention only if he wants the full list.

Just as important for credibility: the composite score put a struggling
crypto-payments name (Bakkt) at the top on raw payments-infrastructure
exposure, and the reality check flagged it on declining revenue. Several
small banks landed on the SMB-lending nodes at peripheral exposure. Point
this out, do not hide it: the tool ranks and flags rather than asserting, and
a growth PM filters the declining name out on the reality layer. A too-clean
list would read as cherry-picked; the honest one shows the machinery working.

## Delivery

- Best channel is a warm introduction if anyone in your network touches T.
  Rowe or the Baltimore investment community. Second best is LinkedIn, where
  a note like this from a builder stands out. Cold email to a work address is
  the last resort and the weakest.
- Attach or link the one-page map, not a deck. The artifact is the proof.
- If it lands and a conversation starts, let it be about the ideas and the
  work. The seat comes up when he raises it, not when you do.
- One touch. This is not the 12-per-quarter cadence; it is a single, careful,
  high-craft approach to the person whose read matters most. If no reply,
  one gentle follow-up after two weeks, then leave it.

## Honesty guardrails

Everything in the map is public and filing-cited. Nothing in the note claims
a track record you do not have or a relationship that does not exist. The
note states plainly that you want to do this work; it does not pretend the
outreach is disinterested product feedback. That transparency is the point.
