# Corollary GTM: the map-as-gift loop

Built from the vault: the Mosaic GTM Operating Plan's signal loop, the
MoreMediaCo gift frame, the Atlas letter voice, and the Marketing Bot Skill
Library (TTFV, willingness-to-pay, 90-day rollout). Sources are Russ's own
systems; this plan is those systems pointed at hedge funds.

## The core insight

Your 13F idea is correct, and it is the same system you already run for
Mosaic LIHTC outreach: enrich a target, classify their latest signal, and
make the next touch carry the deliverable itself. For Corollary the
deliverable is a scenario map run on THEIR stated view before you ever
email them. The email is not about the tool. The email IS the tool's
output. Time-to-first-value is zero because the value arrives in the
inbox (the vault's CH-02 note says cold email tolerates 3 to 5 days to
first value; this compresses it to zero).

## The loop, step by step

1. **Enrich (signals, all public).** For each fund on the target list:
   - 13F deltas quarter over quarter: new positions, adds, exits. The
     pipeline already parses 13F infotables; a diff of two quarters gives
     "what they got bullish on" without anyone saying a word.
   - What they said out loud: quarterly letters posted publicly, podcast
     appearances, conference panels, interviews. One line is enough:
     "[PM] said in [source] they see [theme]."
   - Classify like the Mosaic plan: Tier 1 has a fresh public statement
     or a sharp 13F rotation this quarter; Tier 2 has a stable stated
     strategy but no fresh signal; Tier 3 has neither (newsletter-only).
2. **Run the map.** Turn the fund's stated theme into a Corollary
   scenario, run it, and pull the two or three most surprising
   under-covered names off the second and third order nodes, with their
   filing sentences. Prefer names NOT in their 13F: the gift is what
   they have not looked at yet.
3. **The touch (Atlas voice, gift frame).** First sentence references
   their specific public statement. Then: "I ran that view through a
   consequence-mapping engine I built. Second order, it points at
   [NAME] and [NAME], both under $2B, thin coverage; the claims trace
   to their own 10-K sentences. The three-node excerpt is attached."
   CTA understated: "If the full map is useful, I will send you a
   sign-in." No feature list, no deck, no "revolutionary."
4. **Escalate on the Mosaic cadence.** Day 1 email plus LinkedIn follow,
   Day 5 second email (one new node from the same map), Day 8 LinkedIn
   engagement, Day 12 final email with the film link and the sign-in
   offer. Pause on any reply. Tier 2 funds get one letter per quarter
   timed to 13F publication, which is a natural, honest excuse to write.

## Top of the funnel, reframed

You asked if you are touching the top of the funnel wrong. The reframe:
for a 12-per-year sales motion, there is no "top of funnel" in the
mass-marketing sense. The funnel is: 154 screened funds (targets.md) ->
maybe 40 with public statements worth mapping -> 12 personalized maps a
quarter -> demos -> design partners. The X account, the film, and the
landing page are not acquisition channels; they are legitimacy checks
that fire in the 30 seconds after a PM reads your email and searches
you. Their job is to not lose the deal, not to source it.

## Pricing and the dependency goal

The vault's willingness-to-pay method says price the value gap, not the
build cost. The job is long-term idea generation for a fundamental
analyst: hours saved per idea surfaced, plus ideas they would not have
found. That supports two honest tiers once out of preview:

- Analyst, $150 a month, self-serve, Google sign-in, capped runs. This
  is the dependency tier: cheap enough that one analyst expenses it
  without asking anyone, which is how the tool gets into the building.
- Desk, $1,500 a month, five seats, IC memo export, alerts, priority
  runs. This is the tier a PM approves after two analysts already use it.

Do not open at $8K or $5K a month; that price requires a procurement
conversation you cannot win without references. Land cheap, expand on
usage. Design partners from the loop above stay free until the deploy is
stable, then convert at the Analyst tier with the Desk offer on the table.

Dependency is a product behavior, not a price point. The hooks that
create weekly habit, in order of power:
1. Alerts on their saved scenarios (new filings landing on their map).
2. The article-link habit: paste a headline, get the bull and bear map.
3. Memo export that drops into their existing IC process.
The 90-day retention KPI from the vault rollout framework applies: if
weekly active use is below 70 percent of seats by day 60 of a pilot, the
dependency thesis is failing and the product needs another hook before
more sales effort.

## 90-day rollout (kill criteria included)

- Weeks 1-4: deploy, DNS, film hosted, Google sign-in live. Build the
  signal sheet for the 40 mappable funds. Send 12 map-gift emails.
  Kill check: under 3 replies from 12, rewrite the touch, not the list.
- Weeks 5-8: demos from replies, 3 design partners target. One public
  map excerpt per week on X (the posting lanes in x-profile.md).
  Kill check: demos that do not convert to weekly use in 2 weeks mean
  the TTFV inside the product is too long; fix onboarding before
  sending more.
- Weeks 9-12: convert design partners to paid Analyst tier, publish one
  anonymized case study, second batch of 12 letters timed to the
  quarterly 13F drop.

## What to build next for this loop (in order)

1. 13F quarter-over-quarter diff report per fund CIK (the parser
   exists; the diff and a clean one-pager do not).
2. A "prospect map" run mode that renders the three-node excerpt as a
   single shareable image, monochrome, filing citations included, sized
   for email. This is the gift artifact and it advertises the brand
   with zero extra work.
3. Alerts on saved scenarios (the dependency hook).

Compliance note: everything in the loop reads public documents (13F
filings, published letters, public interviews). No scraped contact
databases; find the PM's email the same way as targets.md, one at a
time, from public sources. Nothing in any touch is investment advice,
and the excerpt carries the same not-investment-advice line as the memo.
