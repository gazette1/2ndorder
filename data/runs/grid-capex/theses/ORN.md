# ORN: Data center construction tailwind insufficient to offset structural liquidity risk

## Bear case first
Government contracts ($516MM in federal awards) and data center construction demand provide a revenue floor that could sustain the company through near-term headwinds. Management cites growth in concrete segment from data center demand [1] and Navy infrastructure investment [0]. However, the lack of quantified data center-specific revenue, zero insider buying, and negative net cash position suggest the bull case lacks confirmation from capital allocation or management conviction.

## Business today
Orion Group Holdings provides concrete, marine, and pipeline services to government and commercial customers. Revenue was $852.3MM and cash $1.6MM as of December 31, 2025 (SEC XBRL). Services include port facilities, marine infrastructure, and underwater pipeline installation [2][3].

## Chain position
Orion occupies an order-2 at-risk node as a contractor to data center developers and utilities. The theme argues that grid equipment bottlenecks raise developer costs and delay projects, but Orion’s concrete and marine work is exposed to direct project starts, not the bottleneck itself.

## Customer graph
Government: $516.0MM in federal awards, primarily Department of Defense ($86.3MM, $50.0MM, $46.3MM) (USASpending). No named enterprise customers in filings; 12 other filers name Orion. Customer evidence is concentrated in government, with no disclosed data center-specific enterprise contracts.

## Insider and management signal
Open-market insider transactions: net $0 over 0 buys and 0 sales by 0 insiders in trailing 12 months (Form 4). Management language is generic: “data center demand” and “growth in data center construction” [1][5] without quantified targets, committed capex, or specific programs.

## Street view
No sell-side analyst coverage identified. Consensus, mean target, and firm ratings are unavailable. For a $599MM market cap company with $5.7MM daily dollar volume, thin coverage is consistent with low institutional interest.

## Non-linear case
A data center construction slowdown from utility interconnection delays or rising costs could reduce Orion’s concrete segment revenue. High working capital needs and negative net cash (–$21.6MM) would amplify a revenue decline, forcing asset sales or equity dilution. Share count already grew 2.3% in 12 months; further dilution could compound losses.

## Evidence
- Navy investments in Pacific infrastructure, coastal rehabilitation, data center demand [0]
- Concrete segment growth driven by data center demand and expanding client base [1]
- Pipeline and utility marine services including underwater transmission infrastructure [2]
- Port facility capex affected by shipping and vessel needs [3]
- Customer ability to fund capex subject to shipping industry financial condition [4]
- Texas data center construction growth supported by land and power access [5]
- Primary liquidity needs are working capital and capex [6]
- Forecast model assumptions include asset sale proceeds [7]
- Certain arrangements not material to financial condition [8]

## Sizing and survivability
At $5.7MM average daily dollar volume, a small fund would need ~6 trading days to build a position. Net cash is –$21.6MM, and share count increased 2.3% in 12 months. Thin liquidity and negative cash position increase short-term risk and make the stock vulnerable to adverse news.

## What would change our mind
- A Form 4 showing open-market insider buying of at least $100k would signal conviction.
- A filed 8-K or 10-Q disclosing a named data center contract with a specific dollar value would validate the theme.
- A federal award in excess of $50MM from a non-DoD agency (e.g., DOE) would broaden the customer base.
