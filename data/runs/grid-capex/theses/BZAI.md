# BZAI: Low-power AI solutions exposure to grid capex supercycle is indirect and lacks near-term catalysts

## Bear case first
Blaize sells low-power AI hardware that could benefit from rising industrial electricity costs, as customers may seek efficiency. Federal contract wins totaling $211M (USASpending) demonstrate validated demand from government agencies. Insider selling of $826M (Form 4) may reflect personal diversification rather than fundamental deterioration. Cash of $45.8M (SEC XBRL) and recent revenue provide near-term survivability. The grid capex supercycle may not raise industrial power bills quickly enough to harm Blaize’s current revenue base.

## Business today
Blaize sells AI processing solutions for edge computing, emphasizing low power consumption, high efficiency, and minimal size [0]. As of 2025-12-31, the company reported revenue of $38.6M and cash of $45.8M (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position
Blaize occupies the technology adoption node as a supplier of energy-efficient AI hardware. The grid capex supercycle could increase demand if rising power tariffs incentivize customers to adopt low-power systems, but the linkage is indirect and not quantified in filings.

## Customer graph
Government customers account for the majority of known revenue. Federal awards total $211M from the Department of the Interior ($85M, $79M) and Department of Defense ($23M) (USASpending). No named enterprise customers appear in the excerpts. Customer evidence is concentrated in federal contracts, creating dependency risk.

## Insider and management signal
Net open-market insider selling of $826M over the trailing 12 months, with zero buys and seven sales by zero insiders (Form 4). Management language in the filing is generic, describing product attributes without quantified targets or programs tied to the grid capex theme [0].

## Street view
No sell-side coverage from known analysts; consensus rating and mean target price are not available. Thin coverage is typical for a micro-cap and limits external validation of the thesis.

## Non-linear case
The downside scenario compounds if higher industrial electricity tariffs delay customer adoption of new AI systems, reducing near-term revenue. Cash burn at the current rate implies a runway of roughly 2.5 quarters (SEC XBRL). A shelf registration on file (Reality check) enables rapid dilutive financing. If federal contract awards slow or concentrate further, the stock could trade toward net cash of $45.8M or below.

## Evidence

## Sizing and survivability
Average daily dollar volume of $17.2M allows a small fund to build a position in approximately two trading days (Reality check). Net cash of $45.8M provides limited runway. Share count increased 39.9 percent in the trailing 12 months, and a shelf registration is on file (Reality check). Dilution risk is material and threatens the short thesis if the company issues equity.

## What would change our mind
1. A Form 4 filing showing open-market insider buying exceeding $1M.  
2. An 8-K or 10-Q filing naming a new enterprise customer contract with stated revenue value.  
3. A USASpending update showing a federal award over $50M from a new agency, reducing concentration.
