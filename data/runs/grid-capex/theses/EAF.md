# EAF: Grid capex supercycle raises power costs for EAF steel mills, compressing electrode demand

## Bear case first  
The short thesis fails because GrafTech’s current volume trends are stable and its product is indispensable to EAF steelmaking [0]. Higher power costs may be absorbed by customers or passed through to steel buyers, delaying any demand erosion. The company holds $176.9MM in cash (XBRL) and invests only $35MM annually in maintenance capex [1], providing a buffer. Insider selling is zero (Form 4), and no customer concentration risk is disclosed in filings, the federal award data shows just $2.0MM in total (USASpending), a trivial share of $504.1MM revenue. The disconfirming read: the grid capex theme may not materially affect GrafTech’s volumes within the investment horizon.

## Business today  
GrafTech manufactures graphite electrodes, a consumable input for electric arc furnace steelmaking and other ferrous/non-ferrous metal production [3]. For fiscal year 2025, revenue was $504.1MM and cash was $176.9MM (XBRL). Capital expenditures are steady at $35MM, consistent with maintaining current utilization [1].

## Chain position  
GrafTech sits upstream of the at-risk node in the adoption chain. Its customers are electricity-intensive commodity producers (EAF steel mills) that face rising grid-delivered power costs from the US T&D supercycle. Higher COGS for these producers may reduce output or shift production to imports, lowering derived demand for electrodes. GrafTech’s revenue is thus a second-order effect of the theme.

## Customer graph  
Federal awards total $2.0MM (USASpending), all from the Department of Energy. No named enterprise customers appear in filing excerpts. Two other filers name the company. Customer evidence is thin; no single large customer is identified, and no contract awards beyond the DOE grants are disclosed.

## Insider and management signal  
No open-market insider purchases or sales in the trailing 12 months (Form 4). Management language is generic: “current industry-wide pricing levels do not reflect the indispensable nature” of the product [0], but no quantified targets, margin goals, or volume commitments are given. Zero insider conviction.

## Street view  
Sell-side coverage is not reported in available sources. Analyst count, consensus estimates, and mean target are all n/a. Thin coverage for a $250MM float name means limited institutional attention and potentially inefficient pricing.

## Non-linear case  
The downside path compounds if the grid capex supercycle accelerates faster than expected, causing large, sustained increases in power costs for EAF mills. Marginal producers idle or close, reducing electrode demand sharply. GrafTech’s fixed cost base (maintenance capex) and cash burn lead to negative free cash flow [5] and eventual balance sheet stress. Without new catalysts or volume growth, the stock de-rates toward asset value or lower.

## Evidence  
- Volume trends are stable, and current pricing does not reflect the product’s indispensable nature [0].  
- Full-year capital expenditures are anticipated at approximately $35MM, consistent with maintenance levels [1].  
- The shift toward EAF steelmaking and needle coke demand in batteries are expected to support industry growth [2].  
- GrafTech is a leading manufacturer of graphite electrodes for EAF steel and other metals [3].  
- Forward-looking statements include expectations for volume, pricing, revenue, capex, and COGS [4].  
- Free cash flow is defined as operating cash flow less capex [5].

## What would change our mind  
1. A filing (8-K or 10-Q) reporting a sequential decline in customer volumes or a major customer bankruptcy.  
2. Insider open-market sales appear on Form 4, signaling negative conviction.  
3. Management explicitly warns that higher power costs are reducing demand or pricing power in the earnings call or MD&A.
