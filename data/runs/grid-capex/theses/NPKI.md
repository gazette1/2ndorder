# NPKI: Grid supercycle equipment rental play faces insider selling and thin customer validation

## Bear case first
Insiders have sold $6.4MM net in open-market transactions over twelve months with zero buys (Form 4), a strong disconfirming signal when the company is investing aggressively. The customer graph contains no named enterprise buyers, no federal awards, and no other filers name NPKI, the revenue base is unverified beyond the company’s own segment breakdown. If the anticipated grid capex wave slows or utilities self-perform work, NPKI’s fleet expansion and 50% manufacturing capacity increase [2] could strand capital and depress returns.

## Business today
NPKI rents and sells matting and related equipment predominantly to power transmission customers, which contributed 60% of trailing twelve-month rental and service revenues as of 1Q26 [0]. Revenue for fiscal year 2025 was $277.0MM, cash on hand $5.1MM (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position
NPKI is a specialty equipment supplier to electric utilities and their contractors undertaking transmission and distribution upgrades. As regulated utilities expand rate base through T&D spending, NPKI’s rental fleet utilization and manufacturing volumes rise proportionally, capturing a direct slice of each dollar of utility capex.

## Customer graph

## Insider and management signal
Open-market insider activity is net negative $6.4MM across 19 sales, zero buys, trailing twelve months (Form 4). Management language is specific, 2026 capex of $75–$90MM with a $35–$45MM rental fleet expansion and $30–$35MM manufacturing capacity increase [2], but no named customer programs or design wins are cited, leaving the market outlook unvalidated by external signals.

## Street view
No sell-side analyst coverage, consensus, or mean target is reported. The stock is effectively uncovered, which amplifies both information risk and potential upside if an initiating report attracts attention.

## Non-linear case
If the grid capex supercycle runs 2026–2030 as hypothesized, NPKI’s rental fleet and manufacturing capacity, scaled by 50% per the current plan [2], could support revenue growth well above historical run rate. The small revenue base ($277MM) relative to a multi-year T&D spending wave creates the potential for a multiple of current business, provided that NPKI captures and retains market share without disruptive competition. Sustained utility authorization for rate base growth is the necessary condition.

## Sizing and survivability
Average daily dollar volume of $14.8MM means a small fund can build a position in roughly three trading days. Net cash is negative $11.7MM, implying debt exceeds cash, and share count has declined 2.4% over twelve months (no dilution threat indicated). The negative net cash position is manageable but provides no cushion if the capex cycle disappoints.

## What would change our mind
- A named multi-year customer contract or design-win announcement in a future 8-K or 10-K, confirming revenue visibility.
- Insider open-market buying (Form 4) of $1MM or more within a quarter, signaling insider conviction aligned with the capex ramp.
- A federal award of $5MM or more (USASpending) tied to grid infrastructure, validating a government customer channel.
