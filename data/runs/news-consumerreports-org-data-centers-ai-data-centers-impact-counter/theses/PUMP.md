# PUMP: ProPetro’s pivot into data-center power generation ties its valuation to a gas turbine demand cycle that is likely to be oversupplied, while its legacy oilfield services face structural headwinds.

## Bear case first  
The short fails if PROPWR’s data-center service contracts materialize faster than turbine overcapacity erodes margins. Management has already committed $198.4M in equipment orders, secured $157.3M in Caterpillar financing and a $163.3M lease facility, and raised equity [7][8][9][13]. First revenue arrived in Q3 2025, proving some customer acceptance [2][5]. However, no named customers exist in the filings (Customer graph: 0 named, 0 federal awards), and insider net selling of –$872K over two buys and four sales (Form 4) suggests insiders lack conviction that the new segment can offset legacy decline.

## Business today  
ProPetro provides oilfield services (fracturing, cementing, etc.) and, since December 2024, turnkey mobile power generation (PROPWR) for oil & gas producers, general industrial projects, and data centers [1][4][11]. Total revenue is $1,269.2M; cash is $91.3M (SEC XBRL). The power segment contributed nominal revenue through Q3 2025 [2].

## Chain position  
ProPetro occupies the “Gas Turbine Order Declines” node (order 2, at-risk, horizon long). Its PROPWR business purchases natural-gas turbine generator sets and reciprocating engines, making it a direct end-buyer of gas-turbine equipment [10][7]. Lower data-center electricity demand reduces the need for new turbine capacity, compressing margins for both turbine manufacturers and service providers like ProPetro.

## Customer graph  
*Unproven.* No named enterprise customers for PROPWR; zero federal award dollars; only one other filer (a competitor or supplier) mentions ProPetro. ## Insider and management signal  
Management language is unusually specific and committed, named subsidiary, quantified capex, exact financing amounts [7][9][13], but insider open-market activity is negative: net –$872K (Form 4). The disconnect between bullish theses and insider selling is a caution.

## Street view  
Sell-side coverage is unknown; no analyst consensus or mean target available. The lack of visible research is consistent with an under-covered small-cap where institutional attention is low, amplifying binary outcomes.

## Non-linear case  
If data-center electricity demand reaches only 95GW by 2028, new turbine orders collapse. ProPetro’s $198.4M equipment fleet becomes underutilized, forcing asset impairments. The equity raise ($163.3M offering, January 2026) diluted existing holders by ~18.9% (share count increase) and signals capital needs exceed internal cash flow. Without named customers, a revenue miss forces a distressed equity restructuring.

A small fund can build a position in ~1 trading day ($76.8M ADV). Net cash is $91.3M against a $1,482M market cap, thin buffer. The 18.9% dilution over 12 months and a $163.3M equity raise suggest ongoing capital needs; further deterioration could force a dilutive rescue.

1. A Form 4 showing net insider buying >$500K within six months.
