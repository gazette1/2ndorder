# NWE: Slowing data center load growth exposes limited renewable development upside, but regulated earnings buffer the downside

## Bear case first

The short thesis assumes NWE is vulnerable to reduced demand for new renewable PPAs as data center load growth slows. However, NWE is a regulated electric and gas utility, not a merchant renewable developer. Its existing long-term PPAs [0][1] serve regulated retail load, not hyperscaler anchor tenants. Regulation ensures cost recovery and stable earnings, insulating NWE from merchant power price declines. Insider trading is zero (Form 4), signaling no negative conviction. The bear case fails because NWE’s core earnings are protected by rate base and allowed returns, not contingent on data center-driven demand.

## Business today

NWE is a regulated electric and natural gas utility serving retail customers in Montana, South Dakota, Nebraska, and Wyoming. It generates and purchases power under long-term PPAs for wind, unspecified resources, natural gas, and hydro [0][1][2]. Revenue was $414.3M (SEC XBRL) and cash was $8.8M as of December 31, 2025.

## Chain position

NWE occupies the “Renewable Project Development Slowdown” node (order 2, at-risk, horizon mid). The economic logic is that slower data center load growth reduces demand for new renewable PPAs, hurting project returns and development pipelines. But NWE’s existing PPAs are for regulated supply, not speculative data center-linked development, weakening the mechanism’s impact.

## Non-linear case

The downside path where the scenario compounds: if data center load growth stalls, merchant power prices fall, and NWE’s wholesale sales decline. Regulatory lag could prevent full cost recovery from new generation investments required for reliability. If NWE’s regulated allowed returns are challenged by lower load growth, or if it must write down PPAs, earnings and dividends could be cut. The share price could fall by a multiple of current earnings as investors re-rate a utility with stagnating demand.

## What would change our mind

- New PPA announcements or regulatory filings (e.g., 10-K, 8-K) naming data center load as a driver for new generation investment.
- Insider open-market buying (Form 4) indicating management conviction in the data center opportunity.
- Federal awards naming NWE as a supplier for data center-linked projects (USASpending).
