# SLNH: Mispositioned as a liquid cooling beneficiary, company is a data center developer with no component manufacturing exposure

## Bear case first

The seed thesis assumes Soluna Holdings is a liquid cooling component supplier, but the company is a data center developer that lists liquid cooling components as needed equipment, not as something it makes [4]. Insider selling is severe: one insider sold $495M (Form 4) net open-market over 12 months against one buy. Revenue of $29.7M (SEC XBRL) comes from Bitcoin mining and development activities, not from selling cold plates or pumps. The filings show no design wins, no manufacturing capacity for liquid cooling parts, and no named customers. The company’s own risk factors cite energy efficiency regulations as a threat, not an opportunity [6]. Without a clear path to revenue from the supposed beneficiary node, the thesis fails on mechanism.

## Business today

Soluna Holdings develops and operates data centers, primarily for Bitcoin mining and high-performance computing. Revenue was $29.7M (SEC XBRL) as of 2025-12-31, generated from power purchase agreements and curtailment arrangements [2] [5]. Cash on hand was $462M (SEC XBRL), though net cash after debt is negative at $‑17.4M (SEC XBRL plus reality check).

## Chain position

The seed thesis places Soluna as a liquid cooling component supplier (order 2 beneficiary). However, the company occupies the developer/operator node, not the component supplier node. It uses liquid cooling in its designs (Helix program [1]) but does not manufacture or sell cooling components. The economic logic of rising rack power density benefitting component suppliers does not apply here.

## Customer graph

Soluna has zero named enterprise customers in the filings. The customer graph shows no federal awards (USASpending), no named customers, and only one other filer names the company. Buyers are unproven; the revenue comes from PPAs and Bitcoin mining operations, not from direct customer contracts for data center services. Customer evidence is extremely thin.

## Insider and management signal

One insider sold $495M net open-market over 12 months against one buy (Form 4). Management discusses “new direct liquid cooling technologies” and the “Helix” program but provides no quantified targets, committed capex, or timelines [1]. The language is general and includes risk factors around energy efficiency regulations [6].

## Street view

No sell-side coverage reported. Consensus and mean target are not available. Coverage is nonexistent, which for a $188M market cap company is typical but leaves the thesis without external validation.

## Non-linear case

A plausible non-linear outcome would require Soluna to pivot from developer to a liquid cooling component manufacturer, acquiring or building cold plate and pump production lines. This would need material capex (not mentioned in filings) and customer design wins. Even then, the company would compete against established specialists. The probability is low given the lack of any such plans in the 10-K.

## Evidence

- The company mentions “new direct liquid cooling technologies” and “Helix” program but does not state it manufactures cooling components [1].
- Liquid cooling components are listed as needed equipment, not as products the company sells [4].
- Revenue comes from PPAs and Bitcoin mining, not from component sales [2] [5].
- Risk factors include energy efficiency regulations as a threat [6].

## Sizing and survivability

Average daily dollar volume is $35.3M (reality check), allowing a small fund to build a position in about one day. However, net cash is negative ($‑17.4M) and cash runway is only 0.2 quarters (reality check). Share count increased 1163% in 12 months, and a shelf registration is on file (reality check). Dilution risk is extreme; the company may need to raise capital soon.

## What would change our mind

- A Form 4 filing showing significant insider open-market purchases within a quarter would signal genuine conviction.
- A Form 8-K announcing a manufacturing agreement for liquid cooling components (cold plates, pumps) would provide evidence of the supposed node.
- A federal award (from USASpending) for data center services that includes named customer commitments would validate revenue visibility.
