# INV: Hyperscaler efficiency spend accelerates even as total data center power growth slows

## Bear case first

Insider selling of $4.3MM net over twelve months signals low conviction from those closest to the business. Customer evidence is near-zero: no named enterprise or government buyer, no federal award dollars, and only one other filer mentions the company. Revenue of $2.1MM against a $405MM market cap implies extreme valuation on unproven commercial traction. Dilution risk is material, share count rose 57% in twelve months and a shelf registration is on file, and cash runway of roughly three quarters leaves little margin for deployment delays.

## Business today

Innventure owns Accelsius, which sells two-phase direct-to-chip liquid cooling systems for data centers. Revenue was $2.1MM for the most recent fiscal year, with cash of $60.4MM (net cash $52.1MM) (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position

The company sits in the Server Energy Efficiency Suppliers node. Hyperscalers facing power grid constraints invest in efficient cooling to lower electricity per compute, driving demand for products like the NeuCool IR150. Even if total data center electricity demand grows slowly, spending on efficiency retrofits accelerates, creating a tailwind for suppliers with differentiated technology.

## Customer graph

No named enterprise customers and no federal award dollars appear in the excerpts. One other SEC filer names the company. Customer evidence is thin; the filing itself targets hyperscale operators, neocloud providers, and enterprises but provides no signed contracts. (USASpending, customer graph)

## Insider and management signal

Insiders sold $4.3MM net on open market over twelve months, with five buys and six sales by five insiders (Form 4). Management language is confident: "industry's first fully integrated," "true plug-and-play," "wrong bet" on competing single-phase cooling [0][1][4]. The dissonance between insider selling and management rhetoric is a red flag.

## Street view

No sell-side analyst coverage; consensus and mean target are not available. The stock is under-covered, which offers potential upside if coverage initiates but also reflects low institutional interest.

## Non-linear case

If the NeuCool IR150 or HyperStart program secures a design win with a hyperscaler, revenue could jump from $2.1MM to tens of millions as the platform scales. The filing claims the IR150 is the first fully integrated rack-level two-phase solution, a position that could capture share in a market currently using single-phase water cooling, which management calls the "wrong bet." For the non-linear case to materialize, at least one named hyperscaler customer must appear in a filing, and cash runway must extend past the deployment cycle.

## Evidence

- [0] Accelsius announces general availability of NeuCool IR150, the industry's first fully integrated rack-level two-phase cooling solution with 150kW capacity.
- [1] The IR150 is a true plug-and-play system making two-phase liquid cooling accessible to enterprises and smaller operators.
- [2] NeuCool HyperStart program supports hyperscale operators and neocloud providers in validating two-phase direct-to-chip cooling for large-scale deployments.
- [3] Accelsius states the industry should plan for waterless and more energy-efficient liquid cooling architectures from day one.
- [4] Traditional single-phase liquid cooling was "the wrong bet" due to leak risk, corrosion, and complexity.
- [5] Single-phase systems cannot be distributed through standard IT channels, limiting access to large operators.
- [6] HyperStart provides a structured path to validate two-phase cooling alongside single-phase approaches.
- [7] CEO Josh Claman joins a panel on gigawatt-scale AI deployments under historic constraints.
- [8] NeuCool platform scales from single racks to entire data centers with best-in-class thermal efficiencies.

## Sizing and survivability

Average daily dollar volume is $14.1MM, so a small fund can build a position in about three trading days. Net cash of $52.1MM funds roughly three quarters of operations at current burn. Share count growth of 57% over twelve months and an active shelf registration pose significant dilution risk that threatens the thesis if the company must raise capital before revenue materializes.

## What would change our mind

- A Form 4 showing net insider buying of at least $1MM within a quarter, indicating insider conviction.
- An 8-K or 10-Q naming a specific hyperscaler as a customer of the NeuCool IR150 or HyperStart program.
- A federal award (USASpending) to Accelsius for data center cooling, providing government validation and non-dilutive funding.
