# LEU: Reshoring of pharmaceutical ingredient manufacturing to the US accelerates under tariff and supply-security policy

## Bear case first
Centrus Energy operates exclusively in uranium enrichment and nuclear fuel, with no disclosed business, facility, or contract related to pharmaceutical ingredient manufacturing [0][1][5]. The seed thesis maps to process equipment suppliers for API plants; Centrus builds centrifuges for HALEU and LEU enrichment, not reactors, dryers, or process control systems for chemical synthesis [2][8]. The insider net selling of $-62.286M over the trailing 12 months (Form 4) and the company’s near-total reliance on Department of Energy awards ($147,951,761, USASpending) for its current revenue stream indicate management conviction is aligned with nuclear fuel, not pharma reshoring. No filing mentions pharma ingredients, supply chains, or related capital equipment.

## Business today
Centrus Energy supplies low-enriched uranium (LEU) and high-assay low-enriched uranium (HALEU) to domestic and international utilities for nuclear reactor fuel [6][7]. For the fiscal year ended 2025-12-31, revenue was $448.7MM and cash was $1,957.2MM (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position
The proposed adoption chain is domestic API plant construction, where equipment suppliers capture upfront capital spending. Centrus occupies the uranium enrichment node of the nuclear fuel supply chain, producing centrifuges and enrichment services. No economic logic connects pharma reshoring to centrifuge manufacturing or nuclear fuel sales.

## Customer graph
Centrus’s primary paying entity is the U.S. Department of Energy, which provided $147,951,761 in federal awards (USASpending), the only named customer from the filings. No enterprise or pharmaceutical customer is identified in the evidence. Customer evidence is thin and entirely government-concentrated.

## Insider and management signal
Over the trailing 12 months, zero insiders bought open-market shares; one sale of $62,286 generated net insider selling (Form 4). Management language in filings focuses on HALEU demonstration, centrifuge manufacturing, DOE contracts, and nuclear fuel supply [0][1][2][5][9], no mention of pharma reshoring or any diversification into chemical processing equipment.

## Street view
Sell-side coverage is unknown; no analyst count, consensus, or mean target is available from the provided source. The absence of coverage for a $2,217MM market cap company is a notable gap but does not support the pharma-reshoring thesis.

## Non-linear case
A multiple of today’s business would require Centrus to enter an entirely new end market, pharmaceutical ingredient processing equipment, with no current technology, facility, or customer base. Such a pivot would demand a material acquisition or licensing deal, neither of which is indicated in filings. Given the company’s focus on scaling centrifuge manufacturing for nuclear fuel [2][17], the probability of a pharma-related outcome is negligible.

## Evidence
- Centrus won a 2019 contract to build a cascade of advanced centrifuges for HALEU demonstration [0].
- Centrus is working with DOE on a long-term lease extension for the American Centrifuge Plant in Piketon, Ohio [1].
- Centrus expects the initial build-out to achieve nth-of-a-kind centrifuge manufacturing costs [2].
- Centrus employs 430 jobs at its centrifuge manufacturing plant in Oak Ridge, Tennessee [3].
- Since 1998, Centrus has provided utility customers with more than 1,850 reactor years of fuel [4].
- Centrus’s current enrichment involves HALEU production and capabilities for advanced nuclear fuel [5].
- LEU is a critical component for nuclear reactor fuel production [6].
- Centrus supplies LEU and its components to domestic and international utilities [7].
- The AC100M centrifuge is the only deployment-ready U.S. enrichment technology meeting national security requirements [8].
- Centrus is pioneering U.S. HALEU production for next-generation reactors [9].
- HALEU is required by many advanced reactor and fuel designs [10].
- HALEU has U-235 concentration between 5% and 20% [11].
- HALEU offers advantages like better fuel utilization and reduced waste [12].
- Lack of HALEU supply is a major obstacle to advanced reactor commercialization [13].
- Centrus is the only company with an NRC license to enrich up to 20% U-235 HALEU [15].
- The war in Ukraine and Import Ban Act increased enrichment prices and demand for domestic capacity [16].
- Centrus initiated design work on a training facility in Piketon and began domestic centrifuge manufacturing for LEU in December 2025 [17].
- HALEU work began under a 2019 DOE demonstration contract [18].
- DOE continues to pursue HALEU availability for the advanced reactor market [19].

## What would change our mind
1. A Form 4 showing insider purchases of Centrus stock, coupled with management commentary on a new pharmaceutical equipment division.
2. An 8-K or 10-Q disclosing a contract with a pharmaceutical company for supply of centrifuges, dryers, or reactors.
3. A federal award from the Department of Health and Human Services or similar agency for pharma-related capital equipment, replacing the current DOE-centric customer base.
