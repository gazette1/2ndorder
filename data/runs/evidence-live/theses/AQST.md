# AQST: Avoid, Company has no exposure to API reshoring; its fate hinges on a single FDA approval
## Bear case first
The short thesis fails because Aquestive does not manufacture or sell pharmaceutical ingredients exposed to tariffs or reshoring. Its entire near-term value depends on FDA approval of Anaphylm, an epinephrine oral film, and the accompanying loan facility [0]. No filing mentions API manufacturing, foreign supply chains, or tariff risk. Insider trading is zero over twelve months (Form 4), and no customer concentration is documented from filings. The company’s business is orthogonal to the reshoring theme, making it an ineffective short for that trade.
## Business today
Aquestive generates revenue from existing commercial drug products (oral film formulations) and is developing Anaphylm, a New Drug Application for anaphylaxis. Revenue was $44,545,000 and cash $31,807,000 as of 2025-12-31 (SEC XBRL).
## Chain position
The company is a specialty pharmaceutical developer, not an API manufacturer. It occupies a downstream node in the drug product value chain, with no economic link to the reshoring of pharmaceutical ingredients.
## Customer graph
No named enterprise customers or federal award dollars appear in any excerpts or filings (USASpending: $0). Zero other filers name Aquestive as a customer. Customer evidence is unproven from available public data.
## Insider and management signal
Open-market insider activity: $0 net, 0 buys, 0 sales by 0 insiders over trailing 12 months (Form 4). Management language in filings is entirely focused on FDA approval milestones and loan terms [0][1][2]; there is no mention of reshoring, API manufacturing, or tariffs.
## Street view
Sell-side coverage is absent from the dossier: unknown analysts, no consensus, no mean target. For a small-cap with $524MM market cap, this thin coverage implies low institutional attention and high information risk.
## Non-linear case
The downside scenario compounds if Anaphylm fails to receive FDA approval by December 31, 2027. Under the credit agreement, failure triggers mandatory prepayments, reduces available borrowing, and may force a change of control [1][3][5]. Combined with a cash runway of only 2.4 quarters and a net cash deficit of $13,193,000 (reality check), the company would face severe liquidity pressure, likely requiring dilutive equity or a distressed sale. Share count already rose 26.3% in twelve months, and a shelf registration is on file, amplifying dilution risk.
## Evidence
- The Tranche B Term Loan is contingent on FDA approval of Anaphylm before June 30, 2027 [0].
- The loan amount may be reduced if FDA approval is not received by December 31, 2027 [1].
- Exit fee and prepayment premium reductions are tied to net sales milestones or failure to obtain FDA approval [2][3].
- The company must maintain minimum cash, which lowers after Tranche B funding or if FDA approval is not received by December 31, 2027 [4].
- Lenders may require partial loan repayment if Anaphylm is not approved by December 31, 2027 [5].
## Sizing and survivability
A small fund could build a position in about 5 trading days given average daily dollar volume of $7.4MM (reality check). However, net cash is negative ($-13.2MM), cash runway is approximately 2.4 quarters, and share count grew 26.3% with an active shelf. These factors make the equity highly vulnerable to dilution and bankruptcy risk, threatening any short thesis that relies on a stable enterprise.
## What would change our mind
- FDA approval of Anaphylm announced in an 8-K (would remove binary risk and trigger loan funding).
- Insider open-market buying of >$100K reported on a Form 4 (would indicate management conviction in the core business).
- A federal award or contract related to API manufacturing disclosed in USASpending or an 8-K (would show unexpected exposure to the reshoring theme).
