# KPTI: A cash-burning biotech with a single approved drug, facing clinical setbacks and dilution, with no exposure to the reshoring theme.

## Bear case first
Why the short fails: The company retains a commercial product generating $146MM in revenue (SEC XBRL), providing a baseline of cash flow. Cash runway of roughly 3.2 quarters (Reality check) gives time for cost-cutting or a partnership. The voluntary withdrawal of the DLBCL indication removes a costly confirmatory trial obligation [2]. Insider activity shows zero open-market selling (Form 4), suggesting no panic. The short thesis that Karyopharm will run out of cash or face a binary event is not yet confirmed; the stock could trade sideways on low volume.

## Business today
Karyopharm sells XPOVIO (selinexor), a nuclear export inhibitor approved for multiple myeloma, to hospitals and specialty pharmacies. Revenue was $146,067,000 and cash was $60,540,000 as of December 31, 2025 (SEC XBRL). The company has no generic manufacturing or supply chain business.

## Chain position
The company occupies the innovative-biotech node, reliant on patent-protected drug sales and FDA approvals. The reshoring theme does not apply; active pharmaceutical ingredients are sourced from contract manufacturers, not domestic generic production.

## Customer graph
No named enterprise customers and no federal awards (USASpending). Only one other filer names the company (Customer graph). Customer evidence is thin; revenue is from product sales to distributors and pharmacies, not disclosed individually.

## Insider and management signal
Trailing 12 months: zero insider open-market activity (Form 4). Management discusses tariffs only as a general risk factor in one sentence [5] with no specific plans. The DLBCL withdrawal [2] signals a failed catalyst, and management admits the company may never achieve profitability [3].

## Street view
Sell-side coverage is nonexistent: unknown analyst count, no consensus, no mean target (Street view). Thin coverage amplifies information asymmetry and makes the stock prone to retail-driven moves.

## Non-linear case
The downside path: XPOVIO sales decline faster than expected due to competition from bispecific antibodies [6] and pricing reforms [4]. The company exhausts cash before achieving profitability or a new approval, forcing a dilutive offering at a low stock price. Share count has already increased 162% in 12 months (Reality check); a shelf registration (Reality check) enables further dilution. In this scenario, equity value approaches zero as the company becomes a penny stock or restructures.
