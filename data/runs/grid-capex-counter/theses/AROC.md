# AROC: US grid capex grows at a historical average pace through 2030 as data center electricity demand is largely offset by efficiency gains and on-site generation.
## Bear case first
Archrock fails to capture any data center-related growth because the filings show no connection to that end market. The only data center reference is a director’s independent role at Powell Industries [0]; Archrock’s own business is limited to natural gas compression for the energy industry [1][2]. Insider trading is flat over the trailing 12 months (Form 4), and management language never mentions data centers or on-site generation. Customer evidence is thin, a single $68M federal award from the Department of the Interior (USASpending), and no enterprise customer names exist. Without customer adoption, pipeline contracts, or capital commitments, the thesis that on-site gas generation drives incremental compression demand for Archrock remains unsupported.

## Business today
Archrock is a natural gas compression services provider to U.S. energy industry customers and a supplier of aftermarket services for compression equipment [1][2]. Revenue was $1,489.8MM and cash was $1.6MM as of 2025-12-31 (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position
The thesis positions Archrock as an on-site gas supply infrastructure provider (order 2 beneficiary). The economic logic: data centers installing gas-fired fuel cells or microturbines need dedicated pipeline laterals and compression, which should increase demand for Archrock’s midstream compression services. No filing evidence confirms this logic has materialized for Archrock.

## Customer graph

## Insider and management signal

Management language in the sole 8-K excerpt focuses on generic compression services and makes no mention of data centers, on-site generation, or any strategic pivot toward that market [1][2].

## Street view
Sell-side coverage is unknown. Analyst count, consensus, and mean target are all n/a. The stock is effectively uncovered, which is itself a risk for a thesis requiring near-term catalysts.

## Non-linear case
A multiple of today’s business would require Archrock to capture a meaningful share of data center gas infrastructure contracts. This path needs observable evidence: design wins for pipeline laterals serving data centers, a capital spending program targeting data center customers, or management explicitly allocating resources to on-site generation. None of these appear in the filings.
