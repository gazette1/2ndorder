# AAON: Data center efficiency beneficiary with thin execution evidence

## Bear case first

Insider selling is a decisive negative signal: net open-market sales of $24.4MM from 30 sales against only 4 buys over the trailing twelve months. Management describes a large data center TAM but provides no quantified targets, committed capex, or named customer programs. Customer evidence is thin – no named enterprise accounts, only two old federal awards totaling $129K, and just one other filer citing the company. Without design wins or capacity additions, the data center thesis remains speculative.

## Business today

AAON manufactures semi-custom HVAC equipment including rooftop units, air handlers, condensing units, and parts. Trailing twelve-month revenue was approximately $1,002MM; cash on hand as of 2025-12-31 was $13MM (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position

AAON sits as a supplier of cooling solutions (air handlers, liquid cooling) to data center operators and contractors. The economic logic: if data center electricity demand must be constrained to avoid grid upgrades, operators will invest in higher-efficiency cooling, raising adoption rates for AAON’s BASX product line.

## Customer graph

Federal awards total $129K from two Department of Defense contracts (W912CL12P0063, W91ZLK09P1276). No named enterprise customers appear in any excerpt. One other filer names AAON. Customer evidence is thin and concentrated in a small government segment.

## Insider and management signal

Insiders net sold $24.4MM open-market over the trailing twelve months – 30 sales versus 4 buys (Form 4). Management language from the June 2026 presentation cites a $43B data center construction market and a large TAM for BASX products [1][2][3], but lacks any specific customer commitments or capital allocation plans.

## Street view

No sell-side analyst coverage or consensus target is reported in the available materials. The absence of coverage is consistent with a name that has a small data center revenue base relative to the market opportunity.

## Non-linear case

If U.S. grid constraints or regulatory mandates force data center operators to lower PUE aggressively, AAON’s liquid cooling and high-efficiency air handlers could see adoption rates far above current levels. For this to become a multiple of today’s business, AAON would need to win contracts with multiple hyperscale operators and demonstrate that BASX revenue scales meaningfully above the current run rate. No evidence of such wins exists today.

## Evidence

- Data center buildout spending reached $43B in 2025 [1].  
- Annual data center construction is expected at 8–12 GW, with a 240 GW pipeline [2].  
- BASX’s addressable market is estimated relative to the products it provides to data centers [3].  

Dossier facts: insider net sales $24.4MM (Form 4); cash $13MM (SEC XBRL); federal awards $129K from DoD (USASpending); one other filer names AAON.

## What would change our mind

1. A named customer contract with a hyperscaler or colocation operator, disclosed in an 8-K.  
2. Insider open-market buying of at least $1MM within a quarter (Form 4).  
3. A material new federal award or increase in existing awards above $10MM (USASpending).
