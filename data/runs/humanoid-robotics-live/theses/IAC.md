# IAC: Disruption thesis broken – sold the only exposed business in 2022

## Bear case first

The company exited the light industrial staffing segment in November 2022 by selling Bluecrew (its only staffing asset) to EmployBridge. No filing since that sale mentions humanoid robotics, automation, or any related strategy. Insider selling of $279M over 0 buys and 1 sale in the trailing 12 months signals weak conviction from management or major holders. The current revenue ($2.4B) and federal awards ($1.1B) come from businesses with no disclosed link to robotics or logistics disruption.

## Business today

The filings show only that the company sold Bluecrew in 2022 (8-K). Revenue is $2.4B and cash is $960.2M (XBRL). No filing describes current operating segments, product lines, or customer types beyond federal award recipients.

## Chain position

## Street view

## Non-linear case

The company could use its $960M cash balance to acquire a humanoid robotics developer or a firm building automation solutions for warehouses and factories. A strategic pivot into robotics would create direct exposure to the disruption theme. That would require a Form 8-K announcing an acquisition, a change in business description in the 10-K, or insider buying in the acquiring entity.
