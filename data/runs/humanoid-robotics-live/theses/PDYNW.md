# PDYNW: Humanoid simulation bet has no evidence in the filings
## Bear case first
The company’s product is a general robotic adaptation platform, not a physics simulation or digital twin environment for humanoid training. The only filing evidence [0] describes “dynamic real-time operations on the edge” and “generalizing from past experience,” which is the opposite of the simulation-heavy reinforcement learning pipeline the thesis requires. Palladyne’s revenue is $5.2M (SEC XBRL), and no customer is named in an excerpt; the customer graph shows zero federal awards and zero named enterprise buyers. With no insider buying (Form 4) and no disclosed simulator product, the company is mispositioned for the stated chain node.

## Business today
Palladyne AI Corp. sells software that enables robotic systems to perceive their environment and adapt to changing circumstances through learning on the edge, requiring minimal programming and training [0]. Revenue is $5.2M and cash is $18.2M (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position
The seed thesis assigns Palladyne to the “simulation and training platforms” node of the humanoid robotics adoption chain. The company would need to provide physics simulation or digital twin software licensed to robot developers. The filing evidence describes a general adaptation platform, not a simulation environment, and no investment in simulation tools is disclosed.

## Customer graph
The company has no named enterprise customers in the excerpts. Federal awards total $0 (USASpending). One other SEC filer names Palladyne, but the identity and context are not provided. Customer evidence is thin and unproven.

## Insider and management signal
Over the trailing 12 months, zero insiders bought or sold open-market shares (Form 4). Management language in the filing is descriptive and lacks quantified targets, named programs, or committed capital related to simulation or humanoid robotics.

## Street view
No sell-side analysts cover the stock. Consensus and mean target are unavailable. Thin coverage is consistent with the company’s small market capitalization and lack of thematic visibility.

## Non-linear case
A multiple of today’s business would require Palladyne to pivot its edge-learning platform into a humanoid simulation service, or for humanoid developers to adopt the existing adaptation software at scale. The company would need to secure design wins with major humanoid OEMs and disclose simulation-related revenue. None of those steps are evident in the filings.

## Evidence
- The company’s software is “designed to enable robotic systems to perceive their environment and quickly adapt to changing circumstances by generalizing … from their past experience using dynamic real-time operations ‘on the edge’ … without extensive programming and with minimal robot training” [0].

## What would change our mind
- A filed contract (8‑K or customer disclosure) naming a humanoid robot developer as a paying customer for a simulation product.
- Open-market insider purchases reported on Form 4, indicating management conviction in the humanoid thesis.
- A federal award (USASpending) for research or deployment of humanoid training simulation software.
