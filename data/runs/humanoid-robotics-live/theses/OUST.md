# OUST: Sensor supplier positioned for humanoid robotics, but lacks direct proof

## Bear case first
Ouster's current revenue is driven by smart infrastructure and industrial verticals, not humanoid robotics [2]. The humanoid deployment timeline of 2027-2030 is speculative and may slip. No insider open-market transactions in the trailing 12 months (Form 4) signals low conviction. Customer concentration on three Department of Defense contracts (USASpending) creates dependency risk; enterprise customer names are absent. Without explicit design wins or partnerships with humanoid robot builders, the thesis relies on an unconfirmed demand pull.

## Business today
Ouster sells lidar and camera vision products for Physical AI in industrial, robotics, automotive, and smart infrastructure [3]. As of 2025-12-31, revenue was $169.4MM and cash $67.4MM (SEC XBRL). Quarterly product revenue reached $48MM, up 55% year over year, driven by warehouse automation, yard logistics, and intelligent transportation [2].

## Chain position
Ouster provides the perception layer for autonomous systems, including potential humanoid robots. It is a component supplier upstream of robot integrators; sensor demand scales with robot deployment. The disruption of fixed automation integrators is a downstream effect; Ouster benefits if general-purpose humanoids adopt its sensors at volume.

## Customer graph
Federal awards total $509MM from three Department of Defense contracts (USASpending). No named enterprise customers appear in the filings. Two other filers name Ouster as a supplier. Commercial customer visibility is thin, and government concentration is high.

## Insider and management signal
No open-market insider transactions in the trailing 12 months (Form 4). CEO language emphasizes "strong demand from companies building foundational AI models and advanced robotics platforms" [1] but provides no quantified targets or committed capex. Management conviction is verbal only.

## Street view
No sell-side analyst coverage is listed; consensus and mean target are not available. Thin coverage leaves room for discovery but indicates low institutional attention.

## Non-linear case
If humanoid robotics achieves commercial deployment by 2027-2030, demand for lidar and camera sensors could multiply. Ouster could become a standard perception supplier for multiple platforms, driving revenue to multiples of today's $169.4MM. An acquisition by a larger robotics or automotive firm could also deliver a significant premium. For this path, Ouster must secure design wins and demonstrate production scale.

## Evidence
- Ouster won million-dollar contracts for Ouster BlueCity and secured several million-dollar deals for industrial automation [0].
- CEO stated rapid integration and commercial success of expanded camera vision portfolio, with strong demand from AI and advanced robotics platforms [1].
- Product revenue was $48MM, up 55% year over year and 18% sequentially, driven by warehouse automation, yard logistics, and intelligent transportation [2].
- Ouster describes itself as a leader in sensing and perception for Physical AI across industrial, robotics, automotive, and smart infrastructure [3].

## What would change our mind
- A Form 4 filing showing insider open-market purchases.
- An 8-K announcing a specific design-win contract with a named humanoid robotics company.
- A USASpending record showing federal awards explicitly tied to humanoid robot development (e.g., DoD or DOE programs).
