# SLDP: Humanoid robotics battery enabler? No evidence yet.

## Bear case first

Solid Power’s filings contain zero mention of humanoid robotics. The only reference to end markets is a generic “EV and other markets” [1]. Insider selling of $260,363 (Form 4) with no buys signals a lack of conviction from those closest to the business. Customer evidence is thin: $71.6MM in federal awards from three DOE grants, but no named enterprise customers, and no other filers name the company (USASpending). Without commercial revenue from robotics or a stated strategy, the thesis rests on an assumption the company does not appear to share.

## Business today

Solid Power develops solid-state battery technology for the fast-growing EV and other markets [1]. As of December 31, 2025, the company reported revenue of $17.9MM and cash of $21.6MM (SEC XBRL).

## Chain position

The seed thesis places Solid Power as an enabler in the high-energy-density battery node for humanoid robotics. Humanoids require lightweight, long-duration power that advanced chemistries (solid-state or high-NMC) can provide. However, the chain position is speculative; the company’s filings do not indicate any focus on robotics as a distinct vertical.

## Customer graph

Customers are unproven at the enterprise level. Federal awards total $71.6MM from the Department of Energy (three grants: $50.0MM, $5.6MM, $3.6MM) (USASpending). No named enterprise customers appear in the excerpts. Zero other filers name the company. The customer base is government-funded and lacks commercial diversity.

## Insider and management signal

Over the trailing 12 months, insiders sold $260,363 on the open market with zero buys (Form 4). Management language is generic: “leading developer” and “enabling the next generation” [1] – no quantified targets, committed capex, or named programs related to robotics.

## Street view

Sell-side coverage is absent. Analyst count is zero, consensus is not available, and the mean target is not reported. For an under-covered small cap, this lack of attention is itself a negative signal.

## Non-linear case

The non-linear outcome requires Solid Power to win design-ins from humanoid robot OEMs. If solid-state batteries prove superior in energy density and safety for humanoids, revenue could multiply beyond current $17.9MM. What must be true: the company actively develops robotic-specific cells, achieves qualification milestones, and secures multi-year supply agreements. None of these are visible in the filings today.

## Evidence

- [0] Solid Power announced the appointment of Uwe Breitweg (BMW Group VP) to its board, effective July 1, 2026.  
- [1] Solid Power is developing solid-state battery technology to enable the next generation of batteries for the fast-growing EV and other markets.

## What would change our mind

1. An 8-K or S-1 filing disclosing a design win with a named humanoid robotics manufacturer.  
2. A Form 4 showing net insider buying of at least $100,000 within one quarter.  
3. A federal award (USASpending) explicitly funding robotics battery development.
