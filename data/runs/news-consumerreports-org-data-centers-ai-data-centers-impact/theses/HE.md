# HE: No exposure to mainland data center demand growth; isolated Hawaii utility faces headwinds from storm costs, regulatory constraints, and renewable transition without data center catalyst

## Bear case first

The thesis fails because Hawaiian Electric operates only in Hawaii, a geographically isolated archipelago with no connection to mainland U.S. data center markets. Filings contain zero references to data centers, data center demand, or any strategy to serve such customers [1][2]. The company is instead focused on decarbonization, grid hardening, and recovering from the Maui wildfires, which led to a credit downgrade that impedes new renewable projects [15]. Insider conviction is absent: no insider bought or sold stock in the trailing 12 months (Form 4). Customer evidence is thin: no federal awards, no named enterprise customers, and no other filers name the company (USASpending). Without any filing evidence linking HE to the data center supercycle, the map position is effectively unoccupied.

## Business today

HEI is a holding company whose subsidiaries – Hawaiian Electric, Hawaii Electric Light, and Maui Electric – are regulated electric utilities that produce, purchase, transmit, and distribute electricity to approximately 95% of Hawaii’s population [1][2][12]. Revenue as of 2025-12-31 is $3,086.9MM, cash is $501.8MM (SEC XBRL). The utilities’ stated priorities are carbon emission reduction, renewable energy development, and grid modernization [13][14].

## Chain position

HEI occupies the node of a regulated electric utility in an island market with zero exposure to the mainland data center demand surge. Economic logic: regulated utilities earn a return on capital invested in generation and transmission, but that capital is being deployed for decarbonization and storm hardening, not for serving hyperscale data centers. The geographic isolation prevents any load or price benefit from mainland data center growth.

## Customer graph

The company has no named enterprise customers, no federal award dollars (USASpending), and zero other filers cite it. Its actual customers are residential, commercial, and industrial users on Oahu, Hawaii Island, Maui, Lanai, and Molokai – an unproven dataset for data center demand. Customer concentration is diffuse and not exposed to the theme.

## Insider and management signal

No open-market insider buying or selling occurred in the trailing 12 months (Form 4). Management language in filings centers on decarbonization, storm response costs, credit downgrades, and regulatory compliance [13][14][15]. There is no discussion of data centers, data center demand, or any related growth strategy.

## Street view

Sell-side coverage is unknown: analyst count, consensus, and mean target are not available from aggregators. The absence of published research underscores the stock’s limited relevance to the data center investment theme.

## Non-linear case

The non-linear path would require HE to become a direct beneficiary of data center demand. This could happen if Hawaii attracts large-scale data center facilities – possible only with massive undersea cable investments and cheap renewable power, neither of which is mentioned in filings. Alternatively, HE could acquire a mainland utility with data center exposure, but no capital allocation language supports that. For now, the required conditions are speculative and unsupported.

## Evidence

- Hawaiian Electric’s Q1 2026 net income fell to $35MM from $48MM, driven by higher storm response, insurance, and power supply costs [0].
- HEI’s electric utility supplies ~95% of Hawaii’s population and is focused on decarbonization and grid hardening [1].
- HEI has one reportable segment: electric utility, operating only in Hawaii [2][4].
- The utilities set a 70% carbon reduction goal by 2030 and net-zero by 2045 [13][14].
- Credit downgrade after Maui wildfires hinders renewable project financing [15].
- No data center, hyperscaler, or mainland demand is mentioned in any filed excerpt.

## Sizing and survivability

Average daily dollar volume is $25.9MM, allowing a small fund to build a position in about 2 trading days. However, net cash is negative $1,783.2MM, and share count rose 0.1% over 12 months (SEC XBRL). The credit downgrade and negative net cash raise survivability concerns; the stock is not a high-conviction data center play.

## What would change our mind

- An 8-K or 10-K filing that explicitly references a data center interconnection agreement, power purchase agreement, or capacity reservation for a hyperscale customer.
- A Form 4 showing insider open-market purchases of >$1MM, indicating management conviction in an unrecognized data center opportunity.
- A federal award (USASpending) for grid infrastructure tied to data center load, or a named customer disclosure in a filing.
