# GSHD: No exposure to data center electricity demand; insurance franchise model with thin customer evidence and insider selling

## Bear case first
A short fails because the company holds $34.4M in cash (net cash –$2.1M implies some debt) and has a franchise model that generates cost recovery revenue even if GAAP revenue is zero. Insiders executed 10 buys versus 2 sales in the trailing 12 months, suggesting pockets of conviction. Seven other filers name the company, giving it third-party recognition. The data center theme is entirely absent from filings, so a short thesis built on utility regulatory backlash is misdirected; no near-term catalyst exists to drive the stock down.

## Business today
Goosehead Insurance sells property and casualty insurance through a franchise network. Revenue is split into Core, Cost Recovery, and Ancillary, all non-GAAP measures (XBRL). As of 2024-12-31, reported revenue was $0 and cash $34.4M.

## Chain position
The company occupies no node in the power generation or grid infrastructure adoption chain. Its economics depend on franchise fees and insurance commissions, not utility rate cases. The at-risk designation for utility equity investors is a mapping error; GSHD has no exposure to the seed thesis.

## Customer graph
No named enterprise customers or federal award dollars. Seven other filers name the company (unproven). Customer evidence is thin; the business relies on individual franchisees, not large contracts.

## Insider and management signal
Trailing 12 months: net open-market selling of –$3.5M (Form 4) across 10 buys and 2 sales. Management language focuses exclusively on non-GAAP revenue definitions and does not reference data centers, utilities, or grid investment. Insider dollar selling dominates buying.

## Street view
No sell-side coverage reported; analyst count, consensus, and mean target are n/a. Thin coverage limits catalyst visibility but also reduces short squeeze risk.

## Non-linear case
Downside path: if the franchise model fails to produce GAAP revenue (currently $0), cash burn accelerates and net cash becomes deeply negative. Insider selling could intensify ahead of a future filing that reveals declining franchise sales or rising debt. Without a revenue catalyst, the equity may require dilutive financing. This scenario compounds as non-GAAP metrics obscure fundamental erosion.

## Sizing and survivability
Average daily dollar volume $20.8M allows a small fund to build a position in about 2 trading days. Net cash is –$2.1M, meaning debt exceeds cash. With zero reported revenue, liquidity risk exists. No shelf filing noted, but dilution risk is present if franchise sales falter.

## What would change our mind
1. A Form 4 filing showing sustained open-market insider buying in dollar-positive amounts.
2. A 10-Q or 10-K reporting positive GAAP revenue and net cash.
3. A federal award or named enterprise customer disclosed in a filing.
