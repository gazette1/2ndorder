# COBA: Pre-revenue explorer betting that data center-driven mineral demand will create a business that does not yet exist

## Bear case first
The company has no revenue, no reserves, and no defined feasibility study [9]. Its entire thesis depends on converting exploration targets into mines, a multi-year, capital-intensive process that historically fails more often than it succeeds. The only evidence of commercial validation is an offtake arrangement with Glencore [4], but no volumes, prices, or binding commitments are disclosed. Insider conviction is zero: trailing 12-month Form 4 shows $0 in open-market purchases by any insider (Form 4). Without management or board buying their own stock, the market has no signal that insiders believe the exploration story will deliver value.

## Business today
Chilean Cobalt Corp. is a US-based critical minerals explorer with no revenue and $2.8MM cash as of December 31, 2025 (SEC XBRL). It holds the La Cobaltera and El Cofre cobalt-copper projects in northern Chile, and a rare earth element project called NeoRe [3][7]. All projects are at the exploration stage; the company has not established proven or probable reserves [9].

## Chain position
COBA occupies the “Critical Mineral Mining Companies” node in the adoption chain, a third-order beneficiary of the data center electricity demand supercycle. The mechanism is that renewable buildout to power data centers requires copper, cobalt, and rare earths for turbines, batteries, and grid equipment. COBA’s value depends entirely on converting its exploration assets into production to supply these minerals.

## Customer graph
No named enterprise customers. No federal award dollars (USASpending). One other filer names the company, Glencore, through the offtake arrangement mentioned in the 8-K [4], but that is not a customer paying for product. Customer evidence is thin to nonexistent; the business today sells nothing.

## Insider and management signal
Insider open-market trading over the trailing 12 months: zero buys, zero sales by zero insiders (Form 4). Management language is forward-looking and specific about projects and partners [3][4][5], but without quantified targets or insider purchases, conviction is unbacked by capital allocation. The follow-on investment by Glencore and Madesal [2] provides external validation, not insider confidence.

## Street view
No sell-side analysts cover COBA. Consensus, mean target, and ratings are unavailable. For a $162MM market cap explorer with $23MM average daily dollar volume, this coverage vacuum is typical but leaves the thesis without independent analytical support.

## Non-linear case
The non-linear outcome requires COBA to define a resource, complete a feasibility study, and secure project financing. If La Cobaltera or El Cofre prove economic, the company could emerge as an Americas-centric supplier of cobalt and copper [5] in a market where data center-driven demand tightens supply. The 33% share count increase over 12 months (reality check) suggests dilution is already funding exploration; a discovery would justify that dilution many times over. The path is narrow but the payoff is a multiple of the current enterprise value if any project reaches production.

## Sizing and survivability
Average daily dollar volume is $23MM (reality check), implying about 7 days to build a meaningful position for a small fund, not 1,473 days as stated in the dossier, the 1,473 figure appears to be an error (likely based on shares rather than dollars). Net cash $2.8MM (SEC XBRL) provides roughly 9.7 quarters runway; exploration burn will deplete it faster if no additional capital is raised. Share count increased 33% in 12 months (reality check), indicating ongoing dilution. These do not threaten near-term survival but limit upside for existing holders.

## What would change our mind
1. Insider open-market purchases of at least $100K (Form 4) would signal that management sees value.
2. A definitive feasibility study filed in an 8-K or S-K 1300 disclosure would transform the thesis from exploration to development.
3. A federal award from DOE or DOD (USASpending) for critical mineral supply would provide customer validation and non-dilutive funding.
