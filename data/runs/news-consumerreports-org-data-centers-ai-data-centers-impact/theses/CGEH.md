# CGEH: Behind-the-meter microturbine provider positioned for AI data center power demand supercycle

## Bear case first

The company has zero named customers, zero federal awards, and no other filers name it. Customer validation is absent. Revenue of $106MM is tiny relative to the TAM; without a single disclosed enterprise buyer, the thesis rests on the assumption that hyperscalers will adopt a product line that has no public traction. Insider buying is modest ($295K across six insiders) and could signal a floor rather than conviction. The shelf registration raises dilution risk.

## Business today

Capstone sells microturbine-based power generation systems for behind-the-meter applications, including combined heat and power, microgrids, and waste-to-energy. Revenue was $106MM in fiscal 2026, with $28.2MM cash on hand. Products serve data centers, hospitals, agriculture, and industrial facilities.

## Chain position

Capstone occupies the behind-the-meter generation node (order 2 beneficiary). Data center operators invest in on-site power to bypass grid congestion and ensure reliability; Capstone’s microturbines and integrated microgrid solutions address that need directly. The C250 and Energy Surplus Program are purpose-built for AI data center block-power topologies.

## Customer graph

No named enterprise customers. Federal awards: $0. No other filers name the company. Customer evidence is thin: the filings assert service to “critical industries including data centers” but do not disclose any specific buyer.

## Insider and management signal

Open-market insider buying totals $295K across 6 buys and 0 sales by 6 insiders (Form 4). Management language is specific and product-focused, citing the C250, ESP, CARB certifications, and UL1741 SB / IEEE 1547 milestones, but provides no quantified targets or capex commitments, limiting conviction.

## Street view

No sell-side coverage identified. Consensus and mean target are unavailable. For a $153MM market cap with thin analyst attention, the lack of coverage is a setup for discovery but also a risk of low institutional awareness.

## Non-linear case

If Capstone secures a single hyperscaler pilot or contract, especially for the C250 or ESP, the revenue base could multiply rapidly given the 80–150GW data center demand gap. The modular, plug-and-play design and low emissions certifications simplify permitting. Success would require one or more named customer wins, which in turn would drive stock recognition and potential accretion.
