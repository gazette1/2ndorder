# SLNH: A single-asset wind farm with a cooperative PPA offers no direct exposure to the data center power demand supercycle

## Bear case first
The company’s only operating asset is a wind farm that sells power under a long-term PPA to a single rural electric cooperative, not to hyperscalers or data center operators. There is no evidence in filings of new PPAs, capacity additions, or management commentary targeting the data center theme. Insider net selling of $495K (27 sales vs. 1 buy) over the trailing 12 months signals low conviction (Form 4). Meanwhile, share count increased 1,163% in 12 months, net cash is negative $17.4M, and a shelf registration is on file – all pointing to severe dilution risk and limited runway.

## Business today
Soluna Holdings, through its subsidiary Briscoe Wind Farm, LLC, owns and operates a wind-powered energy generation facility in Texas. Revenue from power and REC sales under a PPA with Golden Spread Electric Cooperative was $6.4M in 2025 (PPA-only) and total revenue per XBRL was $29.7M; cash per XBRL was $462M but net cash per the reality check is negative $17.4M. The wind farm carries net assets of $137M and debt of $44.8M (CarVal) plus $25.3M in related-party debt [16][17].

## Chain position
Soluna is a utility-scale renewable generator, but its offtake is a single cooperative, not a data center or hyperscaler. The economic logic of the seed thesis – data center operators locking in low-cost PPAs – bypasses this firm because it has no available capacity, no development pipeline, and no contractual link to the data center demand wave.

## Customer graph
Named customer: Golden Spread Electric Cooperative (enterprise, cooperative). No federal awards (USASpending). One other filer names the company. Customer evidence is thin: a single customer with a fixed PPA, no hyperscaler or data center relationship.

## Insider and management signal
Trailing 12 months, one insider executed net open-market sales of $495K across 27 sells and 1 buy (Form 4). Management language in the filings is purely financial reporting; no forward-looking statements, quantified targets, or commentary on the data center theme appear in any excerpt.

## Street view
No sell-side analyst coverage is reported by aggregators. Consensus and mean target are not available. The stock is effectively uncovered, consistent with a micro-cap with low institutional interest.

## Non-linear case
For the thesis to work, Soluna would need to secure new PPAs with hyperscalers for additional renewable capacity, or renegotiate its existing PPA to capture higher pricing from data center demand. This would require evidence of new project development, capital raises, or management statements – none of which appear in the filings. Without these, the company remains a single-asset, declining cash flow generator with no catalyst.

## Sizing and survivability
Average daily dollar volume is $35.3M, allowing a position to be built in about one trading day. However, net cash is negative $17.4M, cash runway is only 0.2 quarters, and share count has grown 1,163% in 12 months with a shelf registration on file. These factors threaten survivability and make the name unsuitable for a thesis predicated on long-term demand.

## What would change our mind
1. A new PPA with a named hyperscaler or data center operator (filed as an 8-K).
2. Insider open-market buying (Form 4).
